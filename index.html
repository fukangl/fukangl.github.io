Research

My current research focuses on developing intelligent and autonomous healthcare robots that provide assistance for people to perform activities of daily living. I am also interested in developing methods for robotic manipulation that allow robots to achieve robust and efficient tasks in challenging scenarios.


Our latest work: Bathing Assistance with a Soft Manipulator
Bathing and skin care assistance have been significant parts of nursing care. Our research work aims to develop a soft expandable robotic manipulator to assist people with bathing, especially along the limbs.
We fabricate the soft manipulator using TPU material. Our method uses multidimensional capacitive sensing technique to estimate the relative position of the gripper and the surface of a personâ€™s limb in real time. We use dynamixel motors to control the tightness of the gripper. We mount our soft manipulator onto a mobile stretch robot to do bathing tasks along the human body.

Analysis of Bi-stable Behaviors of FGM Cylindrical Shells
Fukang Liu
This paper presents a theoretical model to predict the bi-stable property of functionally graded material (FGM) cylindrical shells. The condition that the bi-stability exists is given under the principle of minimum potential energy.
